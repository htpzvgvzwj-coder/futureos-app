# FutureOS Product Constitution

| Document status | Phase 2 Bible draft |
| --- | --- |
| Version | 2.0.0-draft |
| Owner | FutureOS product, AI, design, banking, and engineering teams |
| Last updated | 2026-07-13 |

## Product Vision
FutureOS exists to make banking an active partner in the customer's life journey. It does not only display accounts, cards, loans, investments, and insurance. It helps the customer understand where life is heading, what decisions may change the future, and what actions can protect long-term outcomes.

## Product Mission
FutureOS moves OCBC mobile banking from product management to life outcome management through three connected systems: Life Graph, Future Mirror, and Future Self Guardian.

## Core Positioning
FutureOS is an AI-native life management layer embedded in OCBC-style mobile banking. It is not a chatbot, not a product advertisement surface, not a black-box financial engine, and not a replacement for regulated human advice where human review is required.

## Definitions
| Term | Definition |
| --- |--- |
| AI-native life management | A banking experience where AI continuously interprets life context, goals, risks, scenarios, and approved actions. |
| Life outcome | A customer result that matters beyond a transaction, such as family readiness, emergency resilience, home readiness, debt control, investment growth, or retirement security. |
| Autonomous banking | Banking actions prepared or executed by an AI agent only within explicit customer consent, approved limits, audit logs, and reversibility rules. |

## Three-System Architecture
| System | Owns | Must not become |
| --- |--- |--- |
| Life Graph | Customer understanding, live profile, life stage, goals, accounts, products, risks, and detected needs. | A decorative profile card. |
| Future Mirror | Simulation, trade-offs, scenario comparison, future score, uncertainty, and consequence explanation. | A fixed wedding or home calculator. |
| Future Self Guardian | Monitoring, recommendations, action preparation, approved execution, recovery, monthly reporting, and decision memory. | A chatbot personality without accountability. |

## Operating Loop
Understand -> Predict -> Decide -> Execute is the constitutional loop.

- Understand: Life Graph creates a live picture of the customer.
- Predict: Future Mirror compares possible futures.
- Decide: Future Self Guardian recommends the path that protects declared goals.
- Execute: Approved actions are prepared, logged, monitored, and reversible where possible.

## Non-Negotiable Principles
- Life before products.
- Goals before transactions.
- Outcomes before product sales.
- Consent before execution.
- Explanation before trust.
- Recovery before abandonment.
- Long-term partnership before one-time interaction.
- Customer control before automation.
- Guardian accountability before AI confidence.
- Banking responsibility before feature novelty.
- Data responsibility before personalisation.

## What FutureOS Will Never Become
- A generic chatbot.
- A product advertisement engine.
- A black-box financial recommendation system.
- A system that removes customer control.
- A feature collection without architecture.
- A system that treats every customer goal as a home-purchase goal.
- An AI that pretends certainty.
- An AI that manipulates fear.
- A replacement for regulated human advice where human review is required.

## Constitutional Amendment Process
Any change to top-level systems, autonomy levels, consent rules, scoring methods, or product recommendation principles requires a PDR. The amendment must identify affected user trust, AI safety, banking governance, data needs, Singapore context, and rollback path.

## Purpose
Keep FutureOS coherent as it expands beyond the first prototype.

## Scope
Applies to every product, design, AI, banking, and engineering decision.

## Product Belief
A bank-grade AI product needs a constitution because customer trust is easier to lose than to regain.

## Required Behaviours
- Preserve the three-system architecture.
- Explain decisions before asking for trust.
- Show customer control at every execution boundary.
- Make long-term goals visible before product recommendations.

## Forbidden Behaviours
- New top-level modules without PDR approval.
- Autonomy without guardrails.
- Product pushing without suitability reasoning.
- Scores without explanation.

## Design Implications
- Design must surface system accountability, not just attractive cards.
- Use calm language even when warning about risk.
- Make recovery paths as visible as success paths.

## AI Implications
- AI must separate fact, forecast, recommendation, and action.
- AI must show uncertainty and confidence.
- AI must avoid fabricated product facts.

## Banking Implications
- Banking products are coordinated around outcomes.
- Relationship manager escalation must be available for high-stakes decisions.
- Auditability is part of the customer experience.

## Engineering Implications
- Top-level systems should stay stable while internal components evolve.
- Business logic must not be hardcoded to one persona, one product, or one scenario.

## Examples
- A spending alert is constitutional when it explains evidence, connects to goals, and asks for approval before execution.
- A product suggestion is constitutional when it starts from a goal and shows why the product may fit.

## Anti-Patterns
- Adding a Capital Orchestrator tab as a fourth core system.
- Showing a future score without calculation context.
- Using red fear language to force action.

## Decision Checklist
- [ ] Does this preserve Life Graph, Future Mirror, and Guardian boundaries?
- [ ] Does the customer keep final control?
- [ ] Does the feature improve a life outcome?
- [ ] Can the decision be audited later?

## Phase 2 Implications
- Phase 2 should deepen the three systems instead of multiplying modules.
- Every new capability should be located inside one of the three systems.

## Future Evolution
- Future versions may add new channels or regional contexts, but the constitution remains the anchor.
